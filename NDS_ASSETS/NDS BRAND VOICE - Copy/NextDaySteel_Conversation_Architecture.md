## **üéØ CORE CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK**

### **Universal 4-Stage Structure**
All conversations follow this basic flow, adapted by agent type:

**STAGE 1: OPENING** (15-30 seconds)
- Identify self and company
- State purpose of call
- Gain permission to continue

**STAGE 2: QUALIFYING** (60-90 seconds)
- Assess customer type and needs
- Identify decision-making authority
- Gauge interest level and timeline

**STAGE 3: PRESENTING** (30-60 seconds)
- Present relevant value proposition
- Address specific customer needs
- Handle initial objections

**STAGE 4: CLOSING** (30-45 seconds)
- Secure next step commitment
- Arrange follow-up action
- Confirm contact details

## **ü§ñ AGENT-SPECIFIC CONVERSATION FLOWS**

### **CALLBACK SPECIALIST AGENT**
*For scheduled callback campaigns*

**STAGE 1: OPENING**
*"Hi [Name], this is [Agent] from NextDaySteel calling back as we discussed [timeframe/reason]. Is this still a good time to talk about your steel reinforcement requirements?"*

**Permission Check:**
- If YES ‚Üí Continue to qualifying
- If BUSY ‚Üí "When would be better - shall I call back in an hour or would tomorrow morning work better?"
- If CONFUSED ‚Üí "You requested a callback about steel reinforcement materials - does that ring a bell?"

**STAGE 2: QUALIFYING**
*Since this is a scheduled callback, focus on advancing the conversation:*

**Progress Check:**
- "Where are you with your project planning since we last spoke?"
- "Have your requirements become clearer since [last contact]?"
- "When do you need the materials delivered?"

**Authority Confirmation:**
- "Are you still the person handling material procurement for this project?"
- "Is there anyone else involved in supplier decisions we should include?"

**Timeline Assessment:**
- "What's your target start date for construction?"
- "How urgent is this requirement?"

**STAGE 3: PRESENTING**
*Tailored to their recalled interest:*

**Value Reinforcement:**
- "As discussed, we guarantee next-day delivery to mainland UK"
- "We can handle everything from small emergency orders to large project supplies"
- "Companies like Kier Group use us for urgent gap-fill requirements"

**Specific Solutions:**
- Address their previously mentioned needs
- Confirm delivery capabilities to their location
- Mention relevant services (cut-to-length, multiple deliveries, etc.)

**STAGE 4: CLOSING**
**High-Priority Options:**
- "Let me take your requirements now and get our sales team to prepare a quote"
- "I can arrange for our sales director to call you within the hour"
- "Would you prefer me to email you so you can reply with your specifications?"

**Standard Follow-up:**
- "I'll schedule a follow-up call for [specific date] to check on your progress"
- "Let me send you our direct contact details for when you're ready"

### **WIN-BACK MANAGER AGENT**
*For dormant customer re-engagement*

**STAGE 1: OPENING**
*"Hi [Name], this is [Agent] from NextDaySteel. It's been a while since your last order of [specific previous products] - I noticed it's been [timeframe] since we supplied your [previous project type]. How have things been going with your projects?"*

**Relationship Acknowledgment:**
- Reference specific previous orders when possible
- Acknowledge the time gap professionally
- Show genuine interest in their business

**STAGE 2: QUALIFYING**
**Business Status Check:**
- "Are you still actively working on construction projects?"
- "What types of projects are you focusing on these days?"
- "How has your business been since we last worked together?"

**Current Supplier Situation:**
- "Who are you using for steel reinforcement materials now?"
- "Have you been happy with your current suppliers?"
- "Any challenges with delivery or service you've been facing?"

**Future Needs Assessment:**
- "Do you have any upcoming projects that might need steel reinforcement?"
- "When do you typically plan your material requirements?"

**STAGE 3: PRESENTING**
**Service Improvements:**
- "We've made some improvements since you last used us..."
- "Our delivery coverage has expanded significantly"
- "We now offer [new services/capabilities]"

**Relationship Value:**
- "We'd love the opportunity to work with you again"
- "Your previous projects were [specific positive reference]"
- "We value long-term customer relationships"

**Current Capabilities:**
- "We're still offering next-day delivery across mainland UK"
- "No minimum order requirements - same professional service for any size order"

**STAGE 4: CLOSING**
**Re-engagement Options:**
- "Can I arrange for our sales team to visit and discuss your current requirements?"
- "I'll send you our updated pricing and service information"
- "Would you like me to call back when your next project is starting?"

**Future Pipeline:**
- "When roughly is your next steel project likely to be?"
- "I'll make a note to contact you in [timeframe] about upcoming work"

**STAGE 4: CLOSING**

- "Thanks for your time - have a great day"

## **üõ°Ô∏è OBJECTION HANDLING FLOWS**

### **Common Objections and Response Frameworks**

**"We're happy with our current supplier"**
*Response Flow:*
1. **Acknowledge:** "That's great to hear you have a good relationship"
2. **Explore:** "What do you value most about working with them?"
3. **Position:** "We find many customers use us for backup or emergency situations"
4. **Close:** "Can I send you our contact details for urgent situations?"

**"We don't need anything right now"**
*Response Flow:*
1. **Accept:** "I understand - not every call comes at the right time"
2. **Future Focus:** "When do you typically plan your next projects?"
3. **Value:** "Many customers like having our details for when urgent needs arise"
4. **Follow-up:** "Would it be worth calling you back in [timeframe]?"

**"What are your prices?"**
*Response Flow:*
1. **Context Need:** "Pricing depends on several factors..."
2. **Qualify:** "What type of materials are you typically looking for?"
3. **Process:** "Our sales team can provide accurate quotes based on your specifications"
4. **Action:** "I can arrange for them to call you with pricing information"

**"I'm too busy right now"**
*Response Flow:*
1. **Respect:** "I completely understand"
2. **Brief Value:** "We specialize in next-day delivery for busy contractors"
3. **Reschedule:** "When would be a better time to call?"
4. **Alternative:** "Or I can email you information instead?"

**"We only work with local suppliers"**
*Response Flow:*
1. **Understand:** "Local relationships are important"
2. **Clarify:** "Where are you based?"
3. **Capability:** "We deliver nationwide - [specific local reference if applicable]"
4. **Backup Value:** "Many companies use us for emergency or backup supply"

## **üìû TRANSFER PROTOCOLS**

### **When to Transfer Immediately**

**Technical Questions:**
- Any product specification questions
- Engineering advice requests
- Quantity calculations
- Load-bearing requirements

**Complex Requirements:**
- Large project quotations
- Multiple delivery coordination
- Special cutting or processing
- Compliance certification needs

**Hot Prospects:**
- "I need a quote today"
- "When can someone visit?"
- "I'm ready to place an order"
- Emergency/urgent requirements

### **Transfer Language**

**Technical Transfer:**
*"That's a technical question that requires expertise. Let me connect you with our technical team who can give you the exact information you need. Can I arrange for them to call you back within the hour?"*

**Sales Transfer:**
*"You sound ready to move forward. Let me connect you with our sales director who can handle all the details and pricing. Are you available for a call this afternoon?"*

**Emergency Transfer:**
*"This sounds urgent. Let me get you directly to our emergency supply team. Can I transfer you now or would you prefer them to call you back immediately?"*

## **‚è∞ CONVERSATION TIMING GUIDELINES**

### **Optimal Call Duration by Agent Type**

**Callback Specialist:** 3-5 minutes
- Relationship already established
- Focus on advancing the sale
- Longer if taking RFQ details

**Win-Back Manager:** 2-4 minutes
- Nostalgic but professional
- Don't over-explain past relationship
- Focus on current/future value

**Cold Prospect:** 1.5-3 minutes
- Respect their time
- Quick qualification and value
- Don't overstay welcome

### **Stage Timing Targets**

**Opening:** 15-30 seconds maximum
- Get to the point quickly
- Establish credibility fast
- Gain permission to continue

**Qualifying:** 60-90 seconds
- Most important stage
- Don't rush but stay focused
- Ask targeted questions

**Presenting:** 30-60 seconds
- Relevant value only
- Match their expressed needs
- Don't oversell

**Closing:** 30-45 seconds
- Clear next steps
- Confirm arrangements
- Professional ending
